A K-Nearest Neighbours algorithm to classify the diagnosis of breast tumours.

Currently, the medical applications of AI often receive less attention than a lot of other fields. However, the ability, and potential, of AI in regards to medicine is immense. For example, this K-Nearest Neighbours algorithm, which I built using Java for my final project of my second year at university, has the ability to accurately classify breast tumours as benign or malignant. The algorithm shuffles and splits a dataset of over 500 lines and 30 attributes and plots the training data in a 30 dimensional feature space. The data can be visualised according to 2 of those dimensions as shown in the screenshots below. Then, with each of the unclassified examples, the algorithm looks at the nearest classified data points in the feature space and determines the unknown data point as benign or malignant based on probability. It produces an average accuracy of around 96%. Overall, this is quite a simple algorithm. However, what it says about the potential of AI in medicine is very exciting.

News Article Analyzer and Time Series Forecasting for Stocks

Using AI to assist decisions in financial markets has the prospect of being very lucrative. I have created two apps which aim to explore some of the potential of AI in financial markets. The first of the two is for scanning news articles. News articles can be a useful tool in financial markets as they can show the general sentiment of the market, whether people are buying or selling. However, it is hard as a human to trawl through many news articles in a short space of time. This app scans the internet for news articles related to any inputted stock, determines the sentiment of these news articles using natural language processing, then creates visual tools to help the user determine what they should do. For example, a pie chart which says whether people are buying the stock, selling it, or consolidating (THINK), or a word cloud visualisation (images below are both for this app).
